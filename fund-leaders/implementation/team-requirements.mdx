# Team Requirements & Skills

## Implementation Team Structure

Successful Amos implementations require a cross-functional team with representation from key stakeholder groups. The team structure scales with fund size and implementation complexity.

## Extended Team Members

### Data Steward
**Part-time role focused on data quality and governance**
- Data validation and cleansing oversight
- Business rule definition and maintenance
- Data quality monitoring and reporting

### End User Champions
**Representatives from key user groups**
- Requirements validation and feedback
- User acceptance testing coordination
- Peer training and support

### IT Security Specialist
**Ensures security and compliance requirements**
- Security architecture review and approval
- Compliance validation and documentation
- Access control and audit trail setup

## Resource Allocation Guidelines

### Small Funds (< $500M AUM)
- **Core Team**: 3-4 people (25-50% time commitment)
- **Duration**: 8-16 weeks
- **Total Effort**: 15-25 person-weeks

### Mid-Market Funds ($500M - $2B AUM)
- **Core Team**: 4-6 people (50-75% time commitment)
- **Duration**: 12-20 weeks
- **Total Effort**: 30-50 person-weeks

### Large Funds (> $2B AUM)
- **Core Team**: 6-8 people (75-100% time commitment)
- **Duration**: 16-24 weeks
- **Total Effort**: 50-80 person-weeks

## Success Factors

### Team Effectiveness
- Clear roles and responsibilities definition
- Regular communication and coordination
- Adequate time allocation and commitment
- Strong executive support and sponsorship

### Skill Development
- Proactive training and development planning
- Knowledge transfer from Amos experts
- Hands-on experience and mentoring
- Continuous learning and improvement culture
